Education

The Belizean Educational System is based on British and Caribbean Models which divide schools into Primary, Secondary and Tertiary tiers. Children begin their eight years of primary education with two years of «Infant» classes, followed by six «standards». Secondary education is divided into four «forms». The sixth form of 2 years is the equivalent to Junior Colleges (Sixth Form) that provide post-secondary diplomas recognized in most American and Caribbean institutions of higher learning.

The majority of schools are public and funded by both the Belizean Government and The Roman Catholic Church. Belize’s existing literacy rate is about 75 to 90 percent with an approximate fifty percent secondary dropout rate especially in the Toledo District.
